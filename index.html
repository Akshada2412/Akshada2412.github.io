Project 01
Supply Chain · Risk Analytics · Demand Forecasting · Inventory Optimization

Supply Chain Risk Intelligence Platform

Global merchandise trade fell over $1.5 trillion in 2020 (WTO data) — not because supply chain risk appeared overnight, but because nobody was measuring it before the crisis hit. I built the analytical framework that makes that risk visible and quantifiable.

The platform applies the Herfindahl-Hirschman Index (HHI) — the same formula the US Department of Justice uses for antitrust analysis — to a modeled global trade network across 50 countries and 5 product categories, scoring supplier concentration risk in real time. It then layers in demand forecasting, inventory optimization, and a supplier performance scorecard to give procurement teams a complete decision-making infrastructure.

What the platform covers
  • HHI Risk Engine — concentration scoring across 50 countries, 5 categories, 5 years (2018–2022). Flags high-risk supplier dependencies with geo-risk weighting and executive risk register
  • Demand Forecasting — Holt-Winters exponential smoothing model with 95% confidence intervals. Interactive forecast horizon (1–5 years) with projected growth/decline alerts by category
  • Inventory Optimization — safety stock calculation at 95% service level, reorder point analysis, EOQ model, 12-month inventory cycle simulation, and lead time sensitivity analysis
  • Supplier Scorecard — country-level risk scatter plot (concentration vs. geo-risk), performance scorecard table with dynamic risk labeling (Low / Medium / High Risk)
  • Executive Recommendations — 5 data-driven recommendations that update dynamically based on selected category and lead time settings

Project 02
SaaS · Product Analytics · ML · A/B Testing · Cohort Analysis

Product Operations KPI Intelligence System

Most SaaS teams track NPS and session counts and call it product analytics. This platform goes deeper — it combines ML-powered churn prediction, funnel analysis, cohort retention, A/B testing, and DAU/MAU/LTV tracking into a single live dashboard that tells you not just what happened, but which users are about to leave and why.

Built on a synthetic SaaS dataset of 1,000 users across 4 plan tiers, the platform surfaces insights that standard BI tools miss — including the discovery that support ticket volume is the single strongest predictor of churn, outranking session frequency and NPS by a wide margin.

What the platform covers
  • Churn Prediction Model — Random Forest on 8 behavioral features (ROC-AUC 0.883, 80% accuracy). Identified and resolved data leakage during ETL development. Live risk scoring on all 1,000 users with High / Medium / Low risk segmentation
  • Conversion Funnel Analysis — Free → Starter → Pro → Enterprise funnel with stage-by-stage drop-off rates, activation gap identification, and conversion optimization recommendations
  • Cohort Retention Heatmap — 10 monthly signup cohorts visualized as a color-coded retention grid. Surfaces best/worst cohorts and identifies the critical M2 retention cliff
  • A/B Testing Framework — interactive experimentation simulator with statistical significance testing (t-test, p-value, confidence intervals). Go/no-go decision output based on pre-defined thresholds
  • DAU/MAU/LTV Metrics — 90-day engagement trends with stickiness (DAU/MAU ratio), LTV breakdown by plan tier, monthly churn rate table, and north star metric tracking

Project 03
Technical PM · Program Management · AI Deployment · Launch Readiness

AI Launch Readiness Command Center

The hardest PM call isn't technical — it's telling a room full of stakeholders, after weeks of work and mounting pressure, that the system isn't ready. This project simulates exactly that: a full technical program management cycle for deploying an AI-powered visual defect detection system in a manufacturing environment, ending in a data-driven NO-GO launch recommendation.

Built using Hybrid Agile-Waterfall methodology across 7 phases and 6 cross-functional teams, the project demonstrates how pre-defined KPI thresholds — not gut feel — should drive go/no-go decisions. Every recommendation is anchored to quantified data logged in a live PM governance dashboard.

PM governance layer
  • Project Charter + WBS — 7 phases, 25 tasks, 7 milestones, 19-week timeline across Engineering, AI/Data, Manufacturing, Supply Chain, Quality, and Operations teams
  • RAID Log — 9 items tracked across Risks, Assumptions, Issues, and Dependencies. 5 of 8 items were reactive — a key signal the program wasn't ready to go live
  • Risk Register (P×I scored) — 8 risks scored by Probability × Impact. R1 (supplier delay) scored 25/25 — maximum risk — and cascaded into two downstream phases
  • Change Request Log — 7 CRs logged, 6 approved, 1 rejected (scope descope request to hit timeline). Scope protection under deadline pressure = program discipline
  • UAT Governance — Cycle 1 pass rate: 85% vs pre-defined 95% threshold. Data pipeline showing intermittent failures. Recommendation: AT RISK / NO-GO with structured escalation path
